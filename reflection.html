Reflection on my experience in AP Computer Science Principles.

What was your biggest “aha” moment?

After spending a lot of time last year during AP Computer Science A trying to understand java through pure memorization, my biggest “aha” moment came this year while I was learning the principles of Computer Science. I learned that there was a solid way of understanding computer science that made more challenging classes much easier. While I was very lost last year due to confusing terms and misunderstanding the computer’s logic, the skills I learned in this class helped me fill in the gaps in my knowledge.

Which topic or concept was most challenging and how did you overcome it?

Understanding logic gates was challenging to me. It is often difficult to decode what a logic gate is impacting and determining the outcome was hard for me. I overcame this by allowing myself to use paper to visualize the problem that I was solving. This method made complicated logic gates into simple problems because I could keep track of the outputs much easier.

How did your understanding of computer science change throughout the course?

My understanding of computer science was deepend because of the versatility of this course. The topics covered in the course were wide-ranging but went into a good amount of depth for me to understand them. Understanding binary and the way data is stored helped me contextualize computing in an impactful way.

What are your future goals in technology or beyond?

I hope to become an engineer and hopefully use technology to benefit my projects as I work.


Digital Citizenship & Ethics.

Reflection on Ethics:

The prevalence of misinformation and its impact on public opinion provides a very important example of why digital ethics are so important. False information spread online can create dramatic consequences in the real world. Solutions are needed to inform the public of false information and keep society safe. With platforms such as X, users are able to crowdsource a fact-check on posts by leaving “Community Notes.” Implementing technology like this is a first step toward promoting ethical internet use.
